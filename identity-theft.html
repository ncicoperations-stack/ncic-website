Identity Theft Awareness

Identity theft occurs when someone uses another person's personal information without authorization to commit fraud or other unlawful activity.

Common Identity Theft Indicators

Unknown Accounts

New financial or online accounts appear that you did not create.

Unauthorized Transactions

Unexpected charges or withdrawals are detected.

Credential Abuse

Your email, social media, or banking credentials are used by others.

Document Misuse

Government IDs or personal documents are used fraudulently.

Identity Theft Reporting

Identity theft occurs when someone illegally obtains and uses another person's personal information for financial gain, fraud, impersonation, or unauthorized access to services.

Examples include stolen government identification, compromised bank accounts, fraudulent credit applications, email account takeovers, phone number hijacking, and social media impersonation.

If you believe your identity has been compromised, submit a report through the National Cyber Investigation Center reporting portal.

How to Identify Identity Theft

Review account statements regularly.

Monitor credit activity and account notifications.

Change passwords immediately if compromise is suspected.

Report suspicious activity as soon as possible.
