Honeytrap Awareness

Honeytrap schemes involve deceptive relationships or interactions used to manipulate victims into sharing money, personal information, or sensitive content.

Honeytrap Incident Reporting

Honeytrap scams involve criminals using fake identities, romantic relationships, or deceptive social interactions to manipulate victims into sending money, sharing sensitive information, or complying with extortion demands.

These incidents may include romance scams, sextortion, online blackmail, fake dating profiles, social engineering, and threats involving private photographs or videos.

If you have experienced or suspect a honeytrap-related incident, submit a report through the National Cyber Investigation Center reporting portal.

Common Warning Signs

Rapid Emotional Attachment
Requests for Money
Requests for Sensitive Images
Blackmail or Extortion

Protect Yourself

Verify identities before sharing information.

Be cautious of sudden emotional relationships online.

Do not send money to people you have never met.

Preserve evidence if blackmail occurs.
