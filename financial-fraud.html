Financial Fraud Awareness

Financial fraud involves deceptive activities intended to obtain money, financial information, or assets through misrepresentation.

Financial Fraud Reporting

Financial fraud involves the unlawful acquisition of money, assets, or financial information through deception, manipulation, or unauthorized transactions.

Examples include investment fraud, wire transfer fraud, bank account compromise, payment scams, cryptocurrency fraud, credit card fraud, business email compromise (BEC), and unauthorized financial transactions.

If you believe you have been the victim of financial fraud, submit a report through the National Cyber Investigation Center reporting portal.

Common Financial Fraud Schemes

Bank Transfer Fraud

Unauthorized transfers or deceptive payment requests.

Credit Card Fraud

Unauthorized purchases or account misuse.

Investment Fraud

False investment opportunities promising unrealistic returns.

Business Email Fraud

Fraudulent requests for payments using impersonated accounts.

Warning Signs

Unexpected payment requests.

Pressure to act immediately.

Requests for banking credentials.

Promises of guaranteed returns.
