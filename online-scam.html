Online Scam

Learn how online scams operate, recognize warning signs, and submit information for assessment.

Overview

Online scams involve deceptive schemes designed to obtain money, personal information, or account access through fraud, misrepresentation, or impersonation.

Key Facts

Most scams begin through email, social media, or messaging platforms.
Financial loss is one of the most common impacts of online fraud.
Scammers frequently impersonate trusted individuals or organizations.

Common Scam Types

Investment Scam
Romance Scam
Employment Scam
Marketplace Scam
Tech Support Scam
Cryptocurrency Scam

Warning Signs

  • Pressure to act immediately
  • Guaranteed profits or returns
  • Requests for secrecy
  • Unexpected contact from strangers
  • Requests for money or gift cards
  • Requests for cryptocurrency payments
  • Remote access requests

Information Helpful For Assessment

  • Dates of contact
  • Names used by the suspect
  • Email addresses
  • Phone numbers
  • Websites involved
  • Social media profiles
  • Payment information
  • Timeline of events

What Happens After Submission

Report Received ↓ Assessment Review ↓ Case Classification ↓ Authority Referral

Submit an Online Scam Report

If you believe you have encountered an online scam, you may submit information for intelligence assessment.

Before You Submit

Please provide information that is accurate to the best of your knowledge. Submission of a report does not guarantee investigation, enforcement action, or recovery of financial losses. A reference number will be generated after successful submission.

Important Notice

Submitting a report does not guarantee investigation or enforcement action.
